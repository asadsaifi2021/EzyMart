Our Vision

EzyMart is a solution to simplify grocery shopping by offering a convenient and easy access to fresh produce for the local buyers. By providing a pleasant online shopping experience, EzyMart allows customers to browse, select, and purchase quality products from the comfort of their homes or workplaces.

Audience

EzyMart is designed to serve the users as buyers within its local community through a dedicated delivery service. Users outside the delivery area can browse and explore product listings for informational purposes or personal planning.

Values

The use of such digital shopping, like EzyMart, is always a critical improvement in a society where there are groups of people with traveling disabilities who struggle to go to supermarkets to buy daily goods. Not just a solution to such critical problems, it is also a convenient tool for anyone wanting to reduce the time and effort spent on grocery shopping. Such features include a search bar and an account-based system that secures and personalizes customers' accounts to promote a positive and engaging user experience.

Beyond just convenience, EzyMart prioritizes sustainability and community impact. By providing the item information online and an optimized delivery service model, the app helps lower carbon emissions associated with individual travel. Furthermore, EzyMart provides an outlet for local farmers to consumers, simplifying the partnership model by eliminating the friction of logistical hurdles like having a storefront vs. in-house inventory management.

Success Measures

The project is considered to be successful if all the features are implemented and cover all the requirements of a customer in a shopping session. Time execution from users will also be accounted for. Customers should spend 50% less time, compared to shopping in person, creating their final cart.

The project is considered to be successful if the customers’ overall experience and effectiveness are drastically improved compared to the real-life experience of shopping in the supermarket. In particular, we will mainly account for the execution time from the user to create their final cart and make an order in EzyMart. We will collect the relevant data from the team members’ friends and family members, who have no computer science background and are unfamiliar with EzyMart. We expect the number of testers to be around 15 and the average amount of time is 50% less compared to average time shopping in person.
